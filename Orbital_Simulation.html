Orbital Dynamics

A visual comparison between a classic Newtonian closed orbit and a precessing "rosette" orbit starting from the same initial conditions.

The Sun (Center of Mass)

Stationary massive body providing the gravitational field.

Planet 1: Closed Orbit

Follows the standard inverse-square law:

F = G * M * m / r²

In a perfect two-body Newtonian system, orbits are closed ellipses. The planet returns to the exact same point in space after every revolution.

Planet 2: Rosette Orbit

Follows a perturbed force law:

F = (G * M * m / r²) + (C / r³)

Because of the extra term ($1/r^3$), Planet 2 feels a slightly stronger pull when close to the sun. This causes its "perihelion" (closest point) to shift slightly with every orbit, creating the rosette pattern.
