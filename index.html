Newton vs Einstein: Orbital Precession

A visual comparison between a classic Newtonian closed orbit and a precessing "rosette" orbit following General Relativity.

The Sun
Planet 1: Closed Orbit

Follows standard inverse-square law:

F = G * M * m / r²

Orbits are perfect ellipses and retrace their path indefinitely.

Planet 2: Rosette Orbit

Includes Post-Newtonian term:

F = (G*M*m/r²) + (C/r³)

The extra term causes the "perihelion" (closest point) to shift slightly with every orbit, creating the flower pattern.
